# Hot Work

## Overview

Hot work includes welding, cutting, brazing, grinding, and other operations that produce flames, sparks, or sufficient heat to ignite combustible materials.

A hazard assessment must be completed before work begins.

## Main Hazards

- Fire
- Explosion
- Burns
- Flying sparks
- Hot metal
- Welding fumes
- Gas cylinder hazards
- Ignition of flammable materials
- Oxygen enrichment
- Damage to nearby equipment

## Main Recommendations

- Obtain an approved hot work permit when required.
- Inspect the work area before starting.
- Remove movable combustible materials from the area.
- Protect fixed combustible materials with suitable fire-resistant covers.
- Keep suitable fire extinguishing equipment immediately available.
- Assign a trained fire watch when required.
- Inspect welding and cutting equipment before use.
- Check hoses, regulators, cables, connections, and cylinders.
- Secure gas cylinders in an upright position.
- Separate oxygen cylinders from fuel gas cylinders as required.
- Provide adequate ventilation.
- Use local exhaust ventilation where fumes may accumulate.
- Stop work if unsafe conditions develop.
- Inspect the work area after hot work is completed.
- Follow site-specific permit and fire watch procedures.

## Required PPE

- Safety glasses with side protection
- Welding helmet or suitable face protection
- Face shield when required
- Flame-resistant clothing
- Heat-resistant gloves
- Safety footwear
- Hearing protection where required
- Respiratory protection where exposure limits may be exceeded

## Fire Prevention

- Move combustible materials to a safe location where practical.
- Protect combustible materials that cannot be moved.
- Prevent sparks from reaching lower levels, openings, drains, and hidden spaces.
- Do not perform hot work in an explosive atmosphere.
- Do not perform hot work near uncontrolled flammable gas, vapor, liquid, or dust hazards.
- Keep access to fire extinguishing equipment clear.
- Maintain a fire watch when required by the hazard assessment or permit.

## Gas Cylinders

- Keep cylinders secured.
- Keep cylinder valves protected when not in use.
- Do not lift cylinders by their valve protection caps.
- Keep cylinders away from heat, sparks, and electrical circuits.
- Inspect hoses and regulators before use.
- Close cylinder valves when work is completed.

## Applicable Standards

- OSHA 29 CFR 1910 Subpart Q – Welding, Cutting and Brazing
- OSHA 29 CFR 1910.252 – General Requirements
- OSHA 29 CFR 1910.253 – Oxygen-Fuel Gas Welding and Cutting
- OSHA 29 CFR 1910.254 – Arc Welding and Cutting
- OSHA 29 CFR 1910.119(k) – Hot Work Permit for Covered Processes

## Important Notes

- A hot work permit does not replace a hazard assessment.
- Site rules may require stricter controls than the minimum OSHA requirements.
- Confined space hot work requires additional controls.
- Hot work must stop immediately if gas conditions, ventilation, fire protection, or surrounding conditions become unsafe.

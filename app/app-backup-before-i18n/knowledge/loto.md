# Lockout / Tagout (LOTO)

## Overview

Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) is an energy isolation procedure used to protect workers from the unexpected energization, startup, or release of hazardous energy during servicing and maintenance activities.

Hazardous energy includes electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, chemical, thermal, gravity, and stored energy.

## Main Hazards

- Unexpected machine startup
- Electrical shock
- Stored mechanical energy
- Hydraulic pressure
- Pneumatic pressure
- Steam pressure
- Chemical release
- Thermal energy
- Gravity hazards
- Moving machine parts

## Main Recommendations

- Identify all hazardous energy sources.
- Shut down equipment using normal procedures.
- Isolate every energy source.
- Apply approved lockout devices.
- Attach warning tags.
- Release or restrain stored energy.
- Verify zero-energy state before work begins.
- Test equipment to confirm isolation.
- Keep each worker's personal lock in place.
- Never remove another person's lock.
- Remove locks only after work is complete and all personnel are clear.
- Restore equipment using the approved restart procedure.

## Energy Sources

- Electrical
- Mechanical
- Hydraulic
- Pneumatic
- Steam
- Gas
- Chemical
- Thermal
- Gravity
- Stored energy

## Verification

- Try-start equipment after isolation.
- Test electrical circuits using approved instruments.
- Verify pressure is zero.
- Confirm moving parts have stopped.
- Confirm stored energy has been released.

## Required PPE

- Safety helmet
- Safety footwear
- Electrical gloves where required
- Arc-rated PPE where required
- Face shield
- Eye protection
- Hearing protection
- FR clothing when applicable

## Applicable Standards

- OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 – The Control of Hazardous Energy (Lockout/Tagout)
- OSHA 29 CFR 1910 Subpart S – Electrical
- OSHA 29 CFR 1910.333 – Selection and Use of Work Practices

## Best Practices

- Train all affected employees.
- Use standardized locks and tags.
- Verify isolation before beginning work.
- Perform periodic LOTO inspections.
- Maintain accurate LOTO procedures.
- Use group lockout when multiple workers are involved.
- Remove locks only by the person who installed them.

## Important Notes

- Never rely solely on emergency stop buttons.
- Tags alone do not provide physical protection unless specifically permitted.
- Always verify zero energy before touching equipment.
- Every worker must apply their own lock.
